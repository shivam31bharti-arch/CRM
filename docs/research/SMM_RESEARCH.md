# Social Media Management Research

Sources reviewed: Hootsuite, Buffer, Sprout Social, and Later public product/help materials plus 2026 review coverage for social scheduling and content calendar tools.

## Hootsuite

- Composer UI: multi-network composer with account picker, text area, media attachments, link preview, AI writing assist, platform previews, schedule/publish actions.
- Calendar: planner supports list/week/month style review, campaign planning, approval states, drafts, scheduled and published content.
- Analytics: dashboards show posts, reach, engagement, follower growth, clicks, top posts, response performance, and custom reports.
- Platform connection: OAuth-led account connection with workspace/team permissioning and account health states.
- Unified inbox: aggregates comments, DMs, mentions, reviews, and assignments with status filters.
- Multi-platform differences: composer validates channel-specific text/media rules and shows per-platform previews.

## Buffer

- Composer UI: lightweight queue-based composer, channel selector, text/media inputs, channel previews, schedule or add-to-queue flow.
- Calendar: clean calendar and queue list focused on scheduled content and gaps.
- Analytics: post performance, engagement, impressions, reach, clicks, followers, best-time guidance.
- Platform connection: guided OAuth per channel with connected channel list and plan limits.
- Unified inbox: engagement tools focus on comments and replies where supported.
- Multi-platform differences: tailored character limits, preview cards, platform-specific metadata prompts.

## Sprout Social

- Composer UI: publishing workflow supports account picker, asset library, approval workflows, tags/campaigns, message preview, optimal send times.
- Calendar: collaborative publishing calendar with day/week/month planning, message states, campaign labels, and team approvals.
- Analytics: executive dashboards with profile performance, content performance, audience growth, engagement, response SLAs, competitor/listening views.
- Platform connection: OAuth per network, profile groups, permissions, account status.
- Unified inbox: Smart Inbox combines messages across networks, with assignment, tagging, completion, saved replies, and collision detection.
- Multi-platform differences: strong validation and workflow controls for each network, including media constraints and approval routing.

## Later

- Composer UI: visual-first post editor, media library, Instagram-centric preview, captions, hashtags, first comment, link-in-bio support.
- Calendar: drag-and-drop visual planner with month/week views and feed preview.
- Analytics: visual performance, best times, profile growth, post engagement, link-in-bio clicks.
- Platform connection: channel connection with permissions and media publishing limitations surfaced during setup.
- Unified inbox: lighter engagement features than enterprise tools; emphasizes planning and visual content workflow.
- Multi-platform differences: platform-specific visual previews and media rules, especially for Instagram formats.

## Design Implications

- The composer should place platform selection, content, media, preview, validation, and scheduling on one screen.
- The scheduler should offer month, week, and day modes, plus a list fallback for dense content teams.
- Analytics should start with four executive cards, then charts and sortable post performance.
- Social accounts need visible connection state and mock OAuth flows for local development.
- Inbox should feel operational: filters, unread state, reply composer, contact linking, and real-time updates.
